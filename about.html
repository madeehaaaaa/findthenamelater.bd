About

Everything About Bangla is not a project. It is an archive of breath, memory, sound, and soil.

Bangla was never born in classrooms. It grew in the mouths of farmers, boatmen, mothers, poets, rebels, and children who did not know grammar but knew feeling.

We exist to collect what textbooks forget the unfinished sentences, the folk rhythms, the mistakes that accidentally became beauty.

This space holds language, art, music, radio, and letters not as categories, but as living forms.

— A love letter to Bangla
